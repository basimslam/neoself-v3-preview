The science

We would rather be checked than believed.

Most skincare asks for trust. This page is an attempt to make trust unnecessary, by publishing enough that you can decide for yourself, including the parts that do not flatter us.

01

We print the number.

Every active appears at the concentration we actually formulated with, on the label and on the page. Not "contains vitamin C". Eleven percent. A brand that will not tell you the dose is asking you to assume it is high enough.

02

Measured and reported are not the same word.

An instrument reading and a person's opinion are different kinds of evidence, and collapsing them is how skincare marketing works. We keep them apart. If a number came from a device, we name the device. If it came from asking people, we say they agreed. Never that they saw.

03

The sample size goes on the page.

Ours are small. Thirty people, not three thousand. Publishing that is the point: a percentage without an n is a number that has been chosen rather than found, and you cannot tell the difference unless we tell you.

04

We tell you when not to buy.

Every formula lists what it should not be used with, and our reviews are unfiltered, including the two-star ones, which are frequently the most useful. We do not remove reviews for what they say about the product. Only for spam, personal information, or being about something else entirely.

05

This is a category of one, and that is not a boast.

Nothing here is a breakthrough. The actives are well-understood, the doses are unremarkable, and the formulas are not secret. The only unusual thing NEOSELF does is show its work. That should be the floor, not the differentiator, and the fact that it is a differentiator is the problem we are responding to.
